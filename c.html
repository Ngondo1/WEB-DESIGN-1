Workflow

  1. Preliminaries and Site Analysis: As the first stage, we look at the project requirements, the implications of the site and its context on the project, and planning regulations governing the site.
  2. Conceptualization: In this stage, pencil sketches exploring different arrangements and their effects in 3D are done to ensure the optimal design is chosen.
  3. Scheme design: Dimensioned drawings of the chosen design. They show room sizes, wall thickness, window sizes, etc.
  4. Approval drawings: Detailed drawings showing floor finishes, materials specified, building structure, etc.
  5. Construction details: Blow up detailed drawings of building components to ensure precise execution during construction. (Usually done once construction kicks off)
  6. 3D Visualization: From the scheme design stage to completion, 3D images are provided to enable the client to visualize the drawings as we move along. Before approval drawings are complete, a walk-through video of the project is provided.
  7. Project Management: This will cover management of the process of designing and construction on behalf of the client.
